## Numerical Problems

2.1 The magnitude of cross and scalar products of two vectors are $4 / 3$ and 4 , respectively. Find the angle between the vectors.
(Ans: $60^{\circ}$ )
2.2 A helicopter is ascending vertically at the rate of $19.6 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$. When it is at a height of 156.8 m above the ground, a stone is dropped. How long does the stone take to reach the ground?
(Ans: 8.0 s)

2.3 If $|\mathbf{A}+\mathbf{B}|=|\mathbf{A}-\mathbf{B}|$, then prove that $\mathbf{A}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ are perpendicular to each other.
(Ans: $\theta=90^{\circ}$ )
2.4 A body of mass $M$ at rest explodes into 3 pieces, two of which of mass $M / 4$ each are thrown off in perpendicular directions with velocities of $3 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$ and $4 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$, respectively. Find the velocity of 3rd piece with which it will be flown away.
(Ans: $2.5 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$, opposite to resultant velocity vector of two pieces)
2.5 A cricket ball is hit upward with velocity of $20 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$ at an angle of $45^{\circ}$ with the ground. Find its:
(a) time of flight
(b) maximum height
(c) how far away it hits the ground
(Ans: $2.9 \mathrm{~s}, 41 \mathrm{~m}, 10.2 \mathrm{~m}$ )
2.6 A 20 g ball hits the wall of a squash court with a constant force of 50 N . If the time of impact of force is 0.50 s , find the impulse.
(Ans: 25 N s )
2.7 A ball is kicked by a footballer. The average force on the ball is 240 N , and the impact lasts for a time interval of 0.25 s .
(a) Calculate change in momentum
(b) State the direction of change in momentum
[Ans: (a) 60 N s, (b) In the direction of force]
2.8 An aeroplane is moving horizontally at a speed of $200 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$ at a height of 8 km to drop a bomb on a target. Find horizontal distance from the target at which the bomb should be released.
(Ans: 8.08 km )
2.9 Why does range $R$ of a projectile remain the same when angle of projection is changed from $\theta$ to $9^{\prime}=90^{\circ}-\theta$. Also show that for complementary angles of projection, the ratio $R / R^{\prime}$ is equal to 1 .
2.10 A trolley of mass 1.0 kg moving with velocity $1.0 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$ collides with a similar trolley at rest:
(i) after collision, the $1^{\text {st }}$ trolley comes to rest whereas the second starts moving with velocity of $1.0 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$ in the same direction. Show that it is an example of an elasticcollision.
(ii) after the collision, they stick together and move away with a velocity of $0.5 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$. Show that it is an example of an inelastic collision.
2.11 A railway wagon of mass $4 \times 10^{4} \mathrm{~kg}$ moving with velocity of $3 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$ collides with another wagon of mass $2 \times 10^{4} \mathrm{~kg}$ which is at rest. They stick together and move off together. Find their combined velocity.
(Ans: $2 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$ )
2.12 A car with mass 575 kg moving at $15.0 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$ smashes into the rear end of a car with mass 1575 kg moving at $5 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$ in the same direction.
(a) What is the final velocity if the wrecked car lock together?
(b) How much kinetic energy is lost in the collision?
[Ans: (a) $7.67 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$, (b) $2.11 \times 10^{4} \mathrm{~J}$ ]
