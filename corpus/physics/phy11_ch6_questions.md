## Numerical Problems

3.1 A laser beam is directed from the Earth to the moon. The beam spreads over a diameter of 2.50 m at the moon surface. What is divergence angle of the beam? The distance of moon from the Earth is $3.8 \times 10^{5} \mathrm{~m}$.
(Ans: $6.6 \times 10^{-9} \mathrm{rad}$ )
3.2 A car is moving with a speed of $108 \mathrm{~km} \mathrm{~h}^{-1}$. If its wheel has a diameter of 60 cm , find its angular speed in rad s ${ }^{-1}$ and rev s ${ }^{-1}$.
(Ans: $100 \mathrm{rad} \mathrm{s}^{-1}, 16 \mathrm{rev} \mathrm{s}^{-1}$ )
3.3 An electric motor is running at 1800 rev min $^{-1}$. On switching off, it comes to rest in 20 s . If angular retardation is uniform, find the number of revolutions it makes before stopping.
(Ans: 300 rev )

3.4 A string 0.5 m long holding a stone can withstand maximum tension of 35.6 N . Find the maximum speed at which a stone of 0.5 kg mass can be whirled with it in a vertical circle.
(Ans: $5.5 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$ )
3.5 The flywheel of an engine is rotating at 2100 rev min $^{-4}$ when the power source is shut off. What torque is required to stop it in 3 minutes? The moment of inertia of the flywheel is $36 \mathrm{~kg} \mathrm{~m}^{2}$.
(Ans: 44 N m )
3.6 What is the moment of inertia of a 200 kg sphere whose diameter is 60 cm ?
(Ans: $7.2 \mathrm{~kg} \mathrm{~m}^{2}$ )
3.7 A satellite is orbiting the Earth at an altitude of 200 km . Assuming the Earth's radius is 6400 km , calculate the orbital speed of the satellite. (Ans: $7.77 \mathrm{~km} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$ )
3.8 A space station has a radius of 20 m and rotates at an angular velocity of $0.5 \mathrm{rad} \mathrm{s}^{-1}$. What is the artificial gravity experienced by the astronauts on the space station?
(Ans: $5 \mathrm{~m} \mathrm{~s}^{-2}$ )
3.9 A bicycle wheel has an angular momentum of $10 \mathrm{~kg} \mathrm{~m}^{2} \mathrm{~s}^{-1}$ and angular velocity of $2 \mathrm{rads}^{-1}$. Find the value of its moment of inertia.
(Ans: $5 \mathrm{~kg} \mathrm{~m}^{2}$ )
3.10 A diver comes off a board with arms straight up and legs straight down, giving him a moment of inertia of $18 \mathrm{~kg} \mathrm{~m}^{2}$ about his rotation axis. Then tucks into a small ball, decreasing his moment of inertia to $3.5 \mathrm{~kg} \mathrm{~m}^{2}$. While tucked, he makes two complete rotations in 1.0 second. If he had not tucked at all, how many revolutions would he have made in 1.5 s from board to water?
(Ans: 0.6 rev )
